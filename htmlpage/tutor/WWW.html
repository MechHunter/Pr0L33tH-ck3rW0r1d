Where to begin?

Well, if you want to start somewhere, its a good idea to begin with a sound understanding of how the internet manages the ridiculous amount of info it houses and just how it works.

The world wide web (WWW)

The world wide web, better known as WWW, is probably the most widely heard of concept of the internet, but few know exactly what it is.

Generally, the internet is just a network of millions and millions of data banks connected to an even larger, diverse group of devices ranging from laptops to computers to whatever that uses that data;

all those devices can then use a program called a browser, which can speak to the web servers, and make requests for content they need or rather, what the client needs.

once the relevant web server recieves said requests, provided they do not run into any restrictions on the client device ( i.e. Firewalls, encryptions, Permission restrictions, etc.... ), it will generate the related webpage or data and send it to the client device through the aforementioned browser software; in conjunction with the client device, the browser will translate the recieved data to forms viable to the client device.

Everything in this introduction should be considered as foundational knowledge, the more significant contents are the HTML an CSS basics that preceed it.
